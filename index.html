Current pest alerts — Moore County, June
Squash vine borer — peak flight period
High alert · Moths active Jun–Jul. Lay eggs at squash/zucchini stem bases. Wrap stems with kaolin clay or use row covers.
Japanese Beetle — active now
High alert · Emerge Jun–Aug. Attack beans, corn, squash, okra. Hand-pick into soapy water in the morning when sluggish.
Tomato Hornworm — watch foliage
Moderate · Large green caterpillars. Check undersides of tomato leaves. Look for dark droppings as a first sign.
Striped & Spotted Cucumber Beetle
Moderate · Transmits bacterial wilt, which kills cucumbers in days. Use yellow sticky traps to monitor; row covers on young plants.
Aphids — monitor okra & peppers
Low–moderate · Knock off with a strong water spray. Attract ladybugs by planting dill or fennel nearby.

Organic pest & disease control

Insect controls

  • Neem oil — broad-spectrum; spray leaves top and bottom at dusk. Repeat every 7–14 days and after rain.
  • Insecticidal soap — kills aphids, mites, whiteflies on contact. Do not spray in full sun or heat.
  • Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) — targets caterpillars and hornworms only. OMRI-listed organic.
  • Spinosad — effective against beetles, thrips, leafminers. OMRI-listed organic. Very safe for mammals.
  • Kaolin clay — coats leaves and stems as a physical deterrent to cucumber beetles and squash vine borers.
  • Row covers (spunbonded fabric) — most reliable physical barrier against cucumber beetles and vine borers on young plants.
  • Trap crops — plant Blue Hubbard squash at garden edges to lure squash vine borers away from your main crop.

Fungal & bacterial disease

  • Copper fungicide (organic) — effective against bacterial wilt, early blight, downy mildew. Apply preventively in humid NC summers.
  • Baking soda spray: 1 tsp baking soda + 1 qt water + a few drops dish soap — suppresses powdery mildew on cucurbits.
  • Remove and bag infected leaves immediately. Never compost diseased plant material.
  • Water at soil level only — drip or soaker hoses. Overhead watering spreads fungal spores in humid NC summers.
  • Rotate crops yearly — no tomatoes, peppers, or eggplant in the same bed more than once every 3 years.
  • Choose disease-resistant varieties — look for V, F, N, T codes on seed packets (NC State Extension guidance).
Wildlife in Moore County

White-tailed deer are common throughout the Sandhills. Moore County Master Gardeners note that no plant is truly deer-resistant — a hungry deer will eat anything. Deer leave ragged, torn browse marks; rabbits leave clean diagonal cuts near the ground.

Do NOT use mothballs to deter wildlife — this is illegal under federal pesticide law and dangerous to children and pets (NC State Extension).

🦌 Deer prevention
  • 8-foot fence — most reliable barrier. Deer can jump 6 ft but rarely risk a blind landing over 8 ft. Woven wire or polypropylene deer netting both work.
  • Double fence — two 4-foot fences 3–4 feet apart. Deer are reluctant to jump into a narrow enclosed space.
  • Electric fence with peanut butter bait — deer contact the wire, receive a deterring shock, and learn to avoid the area.
  • Putrescent egg solid sprays (Deer Off, Liquid Fence) — reapply every 2 weeks and after rain. Most effective commercial repellent category.
  • Homemade egg spray: 1 egg + 1 cup milk + 1 tbsp dish soap + 1 tbsp cooking oil per gallon of water. Spray foliage and reapply every 2 weeks.
  • White clover perimeter planting — deer graze it preferentially, leaving vegetables alone (reported effective by Moore County gardeners).
  • Interplant lavender, rosemary, sage, and lamb's ear around the garden border — deer dislike their bitter or aromatic foliage.
  • Motion-activated sprinklers — startle deer at night when most active. Can lose effectiveness over time as deer habituate.
  • Contact Moore County Cooperative Extension: 910-947-3188 for depredation permit info if damage is severe.
🐇 Rabbit prevention
  • 2-foot chicken wire fence buried 6 inches underground and bent 90° outward — rabbits dig under flat-bottomed fences.
  • Rabbits leave clean, diagonal cuts at ground level — identify early before a colony establishes.
  • Cayenne pepper spray: 2 tbsp cayenne + 1 tbsp dish soap per gallon of water. Spray around plant bases. Reapply after rain.
  • Hardware cloth cylinders around individual transplants until plants are at least 12 inches tall.
  • Eliminate brush piles, wood piles, and dense shrubs near the garden — rabbits need nearby cover to forage safely.
  • Dried blood meal sprinkled around the garden border repels rabbits and adds nitrogen to sandy Sandhills soil.
🐀 Groundhog & rodent prevention
  • Bury wire mesh 12 inches deep, bent 90° outward underground. Groundhogs are powerful diggers.
  • Top 12 inches of fence should be floppy and unsupported — groundhogs cannot climb past a rolling top section.
  • Live trap and relocate at least 5 miles away. Contact Moore County Animal Control for guidance.
  • Remove fallen fruit, overripe vegetables, bird feeders, and compost near the garden — all attract rodents.
  • Voles: wrap root vegetables and bulbs in hardware cloth cylinders buried in the soil.
  • Castor oil granules around the perimeter repel groundhogs and moles without harming them or the soil.
Sandhills sandy soil fact: Moore County's deep, fast-draining sandy soils lose both water and nutrients quickly — the defining growing challenge for Sandhills gardeners. The good news: sandy soils warm up early, are easy to work, and respond dramatically to organic matter. Get a soil test first — Moore County Cooperative Extension will send your sample to NCDA&CS labs. Call 910-947-3188.
Amending Sandhills sandy soil
  • Compost is the foundation — add 3–4 inches and till to 8–10 inches every season. The single most effective amendment for sandy soil (NC State Extension).
  • Cover crops — fall: crimson clover, hairy vetch, winter rye. Summer: cowpeas, buckwheat, pearl millet. Till in at flowering to add nitrogen and organic matter.
  • Biochar — mixed at 5–10% by volume dramatically improves water and nutrient retention in sandy soils long-term.
  • Aged wood chip mulch — 3–4 inches around plants reduces watering frequency by up to 50% and feeds soil microbes as it breaks down. Use aged chips to avoid nitrogen tie-up.
  • Worm castings — add ¼–½ cup per transplant hole. Improves water-holding capacity and feeds beneficial soil microbes.
  • Raised beds are highly recommended. Fill with: 60% topsoil + 30% compost + 10% vermiculite or perlite.
  • Target soil pH 6.0–6.8. Sandy Sandhills soils are often slightly acidic — add agricultural lime per soil test results.
  • Increase fertilizer frequency by ~20% vs. package directions for sandy soils — nutrients leach out faster (NC State Extension).
Making organic fertilizer at home

Compost tea

  • Fill a 5-gallon bucket ⅓ with finished compost. Add water to top + 1 tbsp unsulfured molasses.
  • Aerate with an aquarium pump for 24–36 hours. Strain through burlap. Dilute 1:10 with water.
  • Apply as a soil drench every 2–3 weeks during the growing season. Especially valuable in leaching sandy soil.

Worm bin (vermicompost)

  • Red wiggler worms (Eisenia fetida) in a ventilated bin with shredded cardboard or newspaper bedding.
  • Feed vegetable peelings, coffee grounds, crushed eggshells, tea bags. No meat, dairy, or oily foods.
  • Harvest dark, crumbly castings every 3–4 months. The highest-quality organic fertilizer you can make at home.

Organic granular blends

  • Blood meal (12-0-0) — fast nitrogen. Use for leafy greens and corn. Also repels rabbits around beds.
  • Bone meal (3-15-0) — slow phosphorus for root development and transplant establishment.
  • Kelp meal (1-0-2) — micronutrients + potassium. Vital in leached sandy soils deficient in trace minerals.
  • Greensand — very slow potassium + micronutrients. Also improves water-holding in sandy soils over time.
  • Fish emulsion (5-2-2) — fast all-purpose liquid. Apply diluted every 2–3 weeks to growing crops.
  • DIY complete blend: 4 parts compost + 1 part blood meal + 1 part bone meal + ½ part kelp meal. Apply 2–3 cups per 10 sq ft, worked into top 4 inches.
